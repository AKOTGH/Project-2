What Is Implicit Bias?

Implicit bias refers to unconscious attitudes or stereotypes that influence how we perceive, evaluate, and interact with others. These biases develop through socialization, media exposure, and cultural norms, often without our awareness.

In educational, professional, and technological contexts, implicit bias can affect hiring decisions, user experience design, algorithmic outcomes, and access to opportunities.

Intersectionality

Intersectionality explains how multiple aspects of identity—such as race, gender, class, sexuality, and ability—interact to shape lived experiences and access to power.

Understanding intersectionality helps us recognize why a single-axis approach to equity often fails to address the complexity of real lives, especially in technology and policy design.
