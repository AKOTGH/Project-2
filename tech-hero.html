Kimberly Bryant

Kimberly Bryant is a technology leader and social entrepreneur best known as the founder of Black Girls Code, a nonprofit organization dedicated to increasing opportunities for Black girls and women in computer science and related STEM fields.

Bryant founded Black Girls Code after noticing a severe lack of racial and gender diversity in technical spaces, including those her own daughter was entering. Rather than accepting these barriers as inevitable, she focused on early intervention—providing young students with coding education, mentorship, and a supportive community long before college or the workforce.

Her work directly confronts implicit bias in education and technology. Many technology pipelines unintentionally favor students who already have access to resources, encouragement, and representation. Black Girls Code challenges these assumptions by demonstrating that talent is universal, while opportunity is not.

Bryant’s leadership is also deeply intersectional. Her programs recognize that race and gender intersect in ways that create unique barriers for Black girls, particularly in environments where they are often underrepresented or stereotyped. By centering these experiences, her work goes beyond surface-level diversity efforts and instead builds long-term structural change.

Since its founding, Black Girls Code has reached tens of thousands of students across the United States and internationally, offering workshops in programming, robotics, game design, and digital media. Bryant’s approach emphasizes confidence-building alongside technical skills, helping participants envision themselves as creators—not just consumers—of technology.
